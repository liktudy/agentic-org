# Boundary Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines responsibility boundaries for `agentic-org`, headquarters departments, companies, and role agents.

If a boundary is unclear, stop and escalate to the Highest Leader.

## Project Boundary

`agentic-org` builds and governs agentic organizations. It does not own long-term domain execution.

Headquarters may:

- define governance standards
- decide reuse or creation paths
- generate or update company assets
- adapt companies to runtime platforms
- process external deliverables

Headquarters must not silently execute company domain work without approval.

## Headquarters Department Boundaries

### Governance

Owns rules, schemas, authority, review policy, lifecycle policy, naming, permissions, and escalation.

Governance may prepare decision requests, but it does not replace Highest Leader judgment.

### Organization Design

Owns reuse/create/merge/close recommendations, company boundaries, department boundaries, role boundaries, and task level.

Organization Design recommends structure. It does not generate final files or execute workflows.

### Workflow Factory

Owns generation of company source files from an approved or pending organization design.

Workflow Factory generates assets. It does not decide whether execution starts.

### Platform Adapters

Own runtime adaptation for Codex, Claude Code, OpenCode, and other platforms.

Platform adapters may change runtime shape, but not organization truth.

### Delivery

Owns external deliverable formatting and format conversion.

Delivery may change presentation, structure, and format. It must not change source conclusions.

## Company Boundary

Companies execute approved domain workflows.

Companies may:

- run approved company workflows
- produce internal artifacts
- request review, delivery, or escalation
- update performance and lifecycle signals through approved rules

Companies must not:

- create, merge, close, or restructure companies by themselves
- bypass execution approval
- change headquarters governance
- publish external deliverables without Delivery Department processing

## Role Agent Boundary

Role agents must have one primary responsibility.

A role agent may recommend work outside its boundary, but must not execute outside its boundary.

Role agents must escalate when:

- responsibility overlaps another role
- permission level is unclear
- output scope expands beyond the assigned role
- repeated review failure occurs
- organization structure may need to change

## Conflict Resolution

When two departments or agents appear to own the same work:

1. Stop execution.
2. Identify the overlapping responsibility.
3. Propose a single owner and supporting roles.
4. Submit the decision to the Highest Leader when impact is structural, permission-related, or irreversible.
